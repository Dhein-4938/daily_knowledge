## Core Concept

> [!info] Definition
> A **black hole** is a region of spacetime where gravity is so extreme that the escape velocity exceeds $c$, making it causally disconnected from the rest of the universe beyond a boundary called the **event horizon**.

The existence of black holes follows directly from [[General Relativity]]: Einstein's field equations,

$$G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu}$$

admit exact solutions describing the curved geometry around a sufficiently compact mass. The simplest is the **Schwarzschild solution** (1916), giving the metric for a static, non-rotating black hole:

$$ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{r_s}{r}\right)c^2\,dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{r_s}{r}\right)^{-1}dr^2 + r^2\,d\Omega^2$$

where $r_s = 2GM/c^2$ is the **Schwarzschild radius** — the event horizon for this geometry. Crucially, crossing the event horizon is locally undetectable; no tidal spike, no flash. The singularity lies strictly *inside*.

---

## Why It Matters

Black holes sit at the intersection of nearly every major branch of physics:

- **Gravity at its extreme** — they are the cleanest laboratory for strong-field GR, where post-Newtonian approximations fail entirely.
- **Thermodynamics and information** — Hawking's result that black holes radiate like a blackbody with temperature $T_H = \hbar c^3 / (8\pi G M k_B)$ fused quantum field theory with gravity, spawning the still-open [[Black Hole Information Paradox]].
- **Galaxy formation** — supermassive black holes ($M \sim 10^6$–$10^{10}\,M_\odot$) sit at galactic cores and regulate star formation via AGN feedback. Sagittarius A* ($\approx 4.3 \times 10^6\,M_\odot$) anchors the Milky Way.
- **Multi-messenger astronomy** — binary black hole mergers are the loudest sources of [[Gravitational Waves]], providing tests of GR in the highly-dynamical regime.

---

## Key Details

1. **Singularity ≠ physical prediction.** The central singularity (infinite curvature) is widely interpreted as a GR breakdown rather than a real feature — a cue that [[Quantum Gravity]] is needed, not a literal infinitely-dense point.

2. **Hawking radiation is cosmologically negligible today.** Stellar-mass black holes ($M \gtrsim 3\,M_\odot$) absorb CMB photons *faster* than they emit Hawking radiation. Only sub-millimeter primordial black holes would be evaporating now.

3. **Hair theorems constrain the phase space.** The **no-hair theorem** states that stationary black holes are fully characterized by just three quantities: mass $M$, charge $Q$, and angular momentum $J$. Real astrophysical black holes are described by the [[Kerr Metric]] ($Q \approx 0$, $J \neq 0$).

4. **Accretion, not the hole itself, is luminous.** Infalling plasma forms a magnetized accretion disk; friction heats it to X-ray temperatures. This powers quasars — some of the highest-luminosity objects in the universe — and is detectable across cosmic distances.

5. **Detection is always indirect.** Event horizons cannot be imaged directly; what the [[Event Horizon Telescope]] captured is the *photon ring* — a bright ring formed by gravitationally lensed emission just outside $r = 3GM/c^2$ (the photon sphere).

> [!tip] The Shadow Is Not the Horizon
> The EHT "black hole image" shows a shadow roughly $2.6\times$ the Schwarzschild diameter, not the horizon itself. The photon sphere at $r = 3r_s/2$ is what sculpts the observable silhouette.

## Current State

> [!warning] Note: this information may be dated
> Active research frontiers include: resolving the **information paradox** (firewalls vs. fuzzball vs. island formula approaches in holography), constraining **primordial black holes** as dark matter candidates, gravitational-wave parameter estimation from LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA observations of compact binary mergers, and near-horizon tests with VLBI arrays.

The island formula from [[Holography and AdS/CFT]] has recently offered a semiclassical derivation of a unitary Page curve, suggesting information is *not* permanently lost — though the mechanism in asymptotically flat spacetime remains contested.
