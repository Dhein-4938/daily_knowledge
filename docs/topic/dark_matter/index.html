Dark Matter✓ reviewed

Science · Cosmology  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

Dark matter is a gravitationally active, electromagnetically inert component of the universe — it neither emits, absorbs, nor scatters photons, yet exerts measurable gravitational influence on baryonic matter and spacetime curvature.

INFO: Operational Definition
Dark matter is inferred wherever the observed gravitational potential $\Phi(\mathbf{r})$ cannot be reconciled with the visible mass distribution $\rho_\text{bary}(\mathbf{r})$ under standard GR. It interacts via gravity and (possibly) the weak nuclear force, but not electromagnetism or the strong force.

In the Lambda-CDM Model, the energy budget of the universe is:

$$\Omega_\text{total} = \Omega_b + \Omega_\text{DM} + \Omega_\Lambda \approx 0.05 + 0.268 + 0.682 = 1$$

Dark matter thus constitutes ~85% of all matter by mass, yet its particle identity remains unknown.

Why It Matters

Dark matter is not a peripheral anomaly — it is the dominant gravitational scaffolding of cosmic structure. Without it, galaxies could not form at the observed rate or scale; the cosmic web of filaments, voids, and superclusters would not exist. It matters across multiple scales:

  • Galactic dynamics: Flat rotation curves of spiral galaxies (Rubin et al., 1970s) require a diffuse halo $\rho_\text{DM}(r) \propto r^{-2}$ well beyond the luminous disk.
  • Gravitational lensing: The deflection angle $\hat{\alpha} = \frac{4GM}{c^2 b}$ (where $b$ is impact parameter) systematically exceeds predictions from visible mass — most dramatically in the Bullet Cluster, where the lensing centroid is spatially offset from the hot gas after collision.
  • CMB anisotropies: The acoustic peak structure in $C_\ell$ spectra is only reproducible with a cold, collisionless dark matter component seeding potential wells pre-recombination.
TIP: The Bullet Cluster as a Smoking Gun
The 1E 0657-558 cluster collision provides near-decisive evidence against MOND-only explanations: the gravitational potential (traced by weak lensing) separates from the baryonic mass (traced by X-ray emission), exactly as expected if dark matter is a collisionless fluid passing through while gas ram-pressure decelerates.

Key Details

  1. Thermal classification: Cold dark matter (CDM) has negligible free-streaming length ($\lambda_\text{fs} \ll$ galactic scales), enabling small-scale structure. Warm DM ($\lambda_\text{fs} \sim$ dwarf-galaxy scales) is invoked to address the Missing Satellites Problem and Core-Cusp Problem.
  1. WIMP miracle: WIMPs (Weakly Interacting Massive Particles) with $m \sim 10$–$10^3$ GeV and weak-scale cross-sections naturally produce $\Omega_\text{DM} \approx 0.27$ via freeze-out. This "WIMP miracle" motivated decades of direct detection searches, now severely constrained by LUX-ZEPLIN and XENONnT.
  1. Axions: Originally proposed to solve the Strong CP Problem, the QCD axion ($m_a \sim 10^{-5}$–$10^{-3}$ eV) is a well-motivated dark matter candidate detectable via resonant cavity experiments (ADMX). Ultralight axion fields ($m \lesssim 10^{-22}$ eV) form "fuzzy" dark matter with quantum pressure suppressing structure below $\sim 1$ kpc.
  1. Primordial black holes (PBHs): Formed before nucleosynthesis, PBHs in the mass window $10^{17}$–$10^{22}$ kg remain viable dark matter candidates (not yet excluded by microlensing or gravitational wave constraints).
  1. Local density: Despite its cosmic dominance, the local dark matter density near Earth is only $\rho_\odot^\text{DM} \approx 0.3$–$0.4 \text{ GeV/cm}^3$ — low enough that the entire dark matter mass within Neptune's orbit is $\sim 10^{17}$ kg, comparable to a large asteroid.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated
Direct detection experiments (LUX-ZEPLIN 2024, XENONnT) have pushed WIMP cross-section limits below $\sigma \lesssim 10^{-47}$ cm² at $\sim 40$ GeV, significantly constraining canonical WIMP models. Axion searches (ADMX, HAYSTAC) are active. Theoretical interest has shifted toward: ultralight/fuzzy DM, self-interacting DM (SIDM) to resolve small-scale tensions, and dark sector models with hidden gauge groups. The Hubble Tension ($H_0$ discrepancy ~5σ) may implicate early dark energy or exotic DM physics, though no consensus exists.

Modified gravity alternatives (MOND, TeVeS, entropic gravity) persist as minority explanations but fail to simultaneously fit rotation curves, lensing, and CMB data without invoking some dark matter component anyway.

Discussion Prompts

  1. The WIMP miracle suggests a deep connection between electroweak symmetry breaking and the dark matter relic density — is this coincidence evidence of a unified framework, or a selection bias in our theoretical priors?
  1. Self-interacting dark matter (SIDM) resolves several small-scale structure tensions simultaneously. What observational signatures would distinguish SIDM with $\sigma/m \sim 1$ cm²/g from CDM in next-generation surveys?
  1. If dark matter is ultimately described by a hidden dark sector with its own gauge bosons and possibly dark chemistry, does the term "dark matter" remain a coherent single category, or does it fragment into a dark Standard Model?
