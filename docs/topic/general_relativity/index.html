General Relativity✓ reviewed

Science · Physics  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

General Relativity (GR) is Einstein's geometric theory of gravitation (1916), recasting gravity not as a force but as the curvature of a pseudo-Riemannian 4-manifold — spacetime — induced by the presence of energy-momentum.

INFO: Einstein Field Equations (EFE)
The governing equations relate spacetime geometry to matter-energy content: $$G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu}$$ where $G_{\mu\nu} = R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}Rg_{\mu\nu}$ is the Einstein tensor (encoding curvature), $T_{\mu\nu}$ is the stress-energy tensor (encoding matter/radiation), $\Lambda$ is the cosmological constant, and $g_{\mu\nu}$ is the metric tensor. Ten coupled, nonlinear PDEs in total.

Wheeler's aphorism distills the bidirectional coupling: "Space-time tells matter how to move; matter tells space-time how to curve." Free-falling bodies follow geodesics — extremal paths in the curved manifold — rather than being "pushed" by a force.

The metric $g_{\mu\nu}$ determines the invariant line element:

$$ds^2 = g_{\mu\nu}\, dx^\mu dx^\nu$$

From this, all geometric quantities — Christoffel symbols $\Gamma^\lambda_{\mu\nu}$, the Riemann tensor $R^\rho{}_{\sigma\mu\nu}$, and ultimately $G_{\mu\nu}$ — are derived.

Why It Matters

GR is not merely a refinement of Newtonian gravity; it is a conceptual restructuring of the nature of space, time, and causality.

  • Astrophysics: predicts black holes, neutron stars, gravitational waves — all confirmed observationally (LIGO 2015, Event Horizon Telescope 2019).
  • Cosmology: GR underpins the ΛCDM standard model, the Big Bang, cosmic expansion, and CMB predictions.
  • Technology: GPS satellites require both special- and general-relativistic corrections (~38 μs/day combined) to maintain meter-level accuracy.
  • Fundamental physics: sets the arena for understanding the large-scale structure of the universe; its incompatibility with quantum mechanics defines one of the deepest open problems in all of physics.
TIP: Newtonian gravity as a limit
GR recovers Newton's inverse-square law in the weak-field, slow-motion limit ($v \ll c$, $\Phi/c^2 \ll 1$). The Newtonian potential $\Phi$ emerges from the $g_{00}$ component: $g_{00} \approx -(1 + 2\Phi/c^2)$. This correspondence principle is what makes GR well-tested even in the solar system.

Key Details

  1. Equivalence Principle: The weak form states gravitational and inertial mass are identical. The strong form extends this: locally, a freely falling frame is indistinguishable from an inertial frame in the absence of gravity. This is the physical seed from which the geometric formulation grows.
  1. Exact solutions are rare: The EFE are highly nonlinear. Tractable exact solutions require symmetry. Key ones:
  • Schwarzschild (1916) — spherically symmetric vacuum; predicts event horizons
  • Kerr (1963) — rotating black holes; astrophysically universal
  • FLRW — homogeneous, isotropic cosmology; backbone of modern cosmology
  1. Gravitational waves: Perturbations of $g_{\mu\nu}$ propagate at $c$ as transverse, quadrupolar waves. To leading post-Newtonian order, the power radiated is given by the quadrupole formula:

$$P = -\frac{G}{5c^5} \dddot{Q}_{ij}\dddot{Q}^{ij}$$

where $Q_{ij}$ is the mass quadrupole moment.

  1. Geodesic deviation and tidal forces: The Riemann curvature tensor directly governs how nearby geodesics accelerate relative to one another — the geometrization of tidal gravity. Spacetime curvature is the tidal field.
  1. Singularity theorems (Penrose–Hawking): Under physically reasonable energy conditions, GR predicts its own breakdown — singularities are generic, not artifacts of symmetry. This signals the theory's incompleteness at extreme densities.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated

Active frontiers as of early 2026:

  • Quantum gravity: Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG) and String Theory remain the leading frameworks, neither yet empirically confirmed. The black hole information paradox (unitarity vs. GR) remains unresolved despite significant progress via holography (AdS-CFT Correspondence).
  • Gravitational wave astronomy: LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA observations have moved from detection to population statistics of compact binary mergers, probing strong-field GR and nuclear matter equations of state.
  • Black hole imaging: EHT results on M87 and Sgr A test the Kerr metric in the strong-field regime; next-generation arrays aim for horizon-scale movies.
  • Cosmological tensions: The $H_0$ tension (~5σ discrepancy between early- and late-universe measurements) may or may not require physics beyond ΛCDM/GR.
  • Post-Newtonian and numerical relativity: LIGO waveform templates rely on high-order PN expansions and full numerical relativity simulations — a major area of ongoing computational physics work.

Discussion Prompts

  1. The EFE admit the Minkowski flat-space solution even in the presence of matter if $T_{\mu\nu}$ is tuned appropriately — what does this reveal about the non-uniqueness of solutions and the role of boundary conditions in GR?
  1. The strong equivalence principle implies that gravity cannot be "screened" or shielded locally, unlike electromagnetism. What are the deep structural reasons for this, and how does it connect to the spin-2 nature of the graviton in linearized GR?
  1. Singularity theorems guarantee breakdown under classical assumptions, yet the Bekenstein–Hawking entropy $S = A/4l_P^2$ suggests black holes are thermodynamic objects with a quantum microstructure. What constraints does this place on any viable theory of quantum gravity?
