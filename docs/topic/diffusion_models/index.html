Diffusion Models✓ reviewed

Tech · Machine Learning  ·  ~3 min read

Core Concept

Diffusion models are a class of latent variable generative models that learn a data distribution by modeling a two-phase stochastic process: a fixed forward process that destroys structure, and a learned reverse process that reconstructs it.

INFO: Formal Definition
Let $\mathbf{x}_0 \sim q(\mathbf{x})$ be a data sample. The forward process adds Gaussian noise over $T$ timesteps: $$q(\mathbf{x}_t \mid \mathbf{x}_{t-1}) = \mathcal{N}\!\left(\mathbf{x}_t;\, \sqrt{1-\beta_t}\,\mathbf{x}_{t-1},\, \beta_t \mathbf{I}\right)$$ where $\{\beta_t\}$ is a variance schedule. As $t \to T$, $\mathbf{x}_T \approx \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{0}, \mathbf{I})$. The reverse process is learned: $$p_\theta(\mathbf{x}_{t-1} \mid \mathbf{x}_t) = \mathcal{N}\!\left(\mathbf{x}_{t-1};\, \boldsymbol{\mu}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, t),\, \boldsymbol{\Sigma}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, t)\right)$$ Training minimizes a variational lower bound (ELBO) on the negative log-likelihood, which reduces in practice to a denoising objective: predict the noise $\boldsymbol{\epsilon}$ added at each step.

Multiple equivalent formalisms unify under this umbrella: DDPMs (Ho et al. 2020), score-based models via Langevin dynamics (Song & Ermon), and continuous-time stochastic differential equations (Song et al. 2021), where the forward process becomes an Itô SDE and the reverse is governed by the score function $\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log p_t(\mathbf{x})$.

Why It Matters

Diffusion models resolved a long-standing quality/diversity trade-off in generative modeling. GANs produce sharp samples but suffer mode collapse and training instability; VAEs are stable but blurry. Diffusion models achieve state-of-the-art sample fidelity with tractable likelihoods and stable training.

Commercially, models like Stable Diffusion and DALL-E 3 combine a diffusion backbone with text encoders and cross-attention, enabling open-ended text-to-image synthesis. Beyond vision, the framework extends to audio (WaveGrad), molecular design (protein structure generation), and reinforcement learning (Diffuser for planning).

TIP: Unifying Insight
The score-matching perspective reveals that a denoising network $\boldsymbol{\epsilon}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, t)$ implicitly estimates the score $\nabla_{\mathbf{x}_t} \log p_t(\mathbf{x}_t)$. This connects diffusion models to classical energy-based models and Langevin MCMC — the generative process is gradient ascent on a learned log-density.

Key Details

  1. Noise Schedule: The choice of $\beta_t$ (linear, cosine, learned) dramatically affects sample quality and training dynamics. The cosine schedule (Nichol & Dhariwal 2021) improves low-frequency structure capture.
  1. Backbone Architecture: The denoising network is typically a U-Net with time-conditional normalization (GroupNorm + sinusoidal timestep embeddings) or a Diffusion Transformer (DiT), which replaces convolutional blocks with self-attention and scales more favorably.
  1. Sampling Speed: Naive DDPM requires $T \approx 1000$ NFEs (network function evaluations). DDIM (Song et al.) reformulates sampling deterministically, reducing to ~50 steps. Consistency models and flow matching push this further, enabling single-step generation.
  1. Latent Diffusion: Running diffusion in pixel space is expensive. Stable Diffusion operates in the latent space of a pretrained VAE, compressing $512{\times}512$ images to $64{\times}64{\times}4$ tensors — a ~48× compute reduction with minimal perceptual loss.
  1. Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG): Conditional generation without a separate classifier. The network is jointly trained with and without conditioning; at inference, outputs are extrapolated:

$$\tilde{\boldsymbol{\epsilon}}_\theta = \boldsymbol{\epsilon}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, \varnothing) + w\bigl(\boldsymbol{\epsilon}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, c) - \boldsymbol{\epsilon}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, \varnothing)\bigr)$$

where $w > 1$ trades diversity for adherence to condition $c$.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated
The field moves extremely fast; architectural frontiers and benchmark records shift monthly.

Active research lines as of mid-2026 include: flow matching (Lipman et al.) as a cleaner interpolation-based alternative to score matching; consistency distillation for near-real-time generation; video diffusion (Sora-class models) scaling temporal coherence; and integrating diffusion sampling into agentic pipelines (e.g., interleaved text-image generation, as in InterleaveThinker). Mechanistic interpretability of what timestep-specific representations encode is an open theoretical frontier.

Discussion Prompts

  1. CFG boosts adherence but demonstrably reduces sample diversity — is there a principled way to navigate the fidelity/diversity Pareto frontier beyond tuning $w$?
  2. Diffusion in latent space relies on a separately trained VAE whose bottleneck is fixed — how does the expressiveness of the latent manifold constrain what the diffusion model can ultimately represent?
  3. The score function $\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log p_t(\mathbf{x})$ is learned implicitly through denoising; can this be exploited for density estimation or anomaly detection without sampling at all?
