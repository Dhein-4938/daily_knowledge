Persistent Homology✓ reviewed

Math · Topology  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

Persistent homology is the central tool of Topological Data Analysis (TDA): it tracks how topological features — connected components, loops, voids, and higher-dimensional holes — appear and disappear as you sweep a scale parameter across a filtered simplicial complex.

INFO: Formal Setup
Given a point cloud $X \subset \mathbb{R}^n$, construct a filtration — a nested sequence of simplicial complexes: $$\emptyset = K_0 \subseteq K_1 \subseteq K_2 \subseteq \cdots \subseteq K_m$$ parameterized by a radius $\epsilon$. Applying the $k$-th homology functor $H_k(-)$ over a field $\mathbb{F}$ turns inclusions into linear maps, yielding a persistence module: $$H_k(K_0) \to H_k(K_1) \to \cdots \to H_k(K_m)$$ By the structure theorem (Zomorodian–Carlsson 2005), this decomposes uniquely into interval summands $[b_i, d_i)$, each encoding a feature that is born at scale $b_i$ and dies at $d_i$.

The collection of intervals is the persistence diagram (or barcode). Long bars correspond to robust topological signal; short bars are likely noise.

Why It Matters

Persistent homology bridges pure algebraic topology and practical data analysis. Its power lies in being metric-agnostic at the level of features: you don't need to choose a single scale, and the output summarizes multi-scale topology in a single diagram.

Applications span many domains:

  • Computational biology — protein folding landscapes, cancer genomics via mapper graphs
  • Materials science — characterizing amorphous solids and porous media
  • Neuroscience — neural firing patterns modeled as simplicial complexes
  • Sensor networks — coverage verification without coordinates
  • Machine learning — topological regularizers, data augmentation, manifold detection
TIP: Stability is the killer feature
The bottleneck stability theorem guarantees: $$d_B(\mathrm{dgm}(f), \mathrm{dgm}(g)) \leq \|f - g\|_\infty$$ where $d_B$ is the bottleneck distance between diagrams. This means small perturbations in input (noise, sampling) cause small perturbations in the persistence diagram — a non-trivial and deeply useful result absent from most topological constructions.

Key Details

1. Filtration constructions

Two workhorses: the Čech filtration (balls of radius $\epsilon$ around each point, nerve taken as the complex — theoretically clean via the Nerve Lemma, but computationally expensive) and the Vietoris–Rips filtration (connect points within distance $2\epsilon$ — cheaper, but approximates Čech only up to a factor of $\sqrt{2}$).

2. The persistence algorithm

Computed via reduction of the boundary matrix $\partial$ to a column-echelon form (elder rule). Runtime is $O(n^3)$ in the worst case, but near-linear in practice for sparse filtrations. Software libraries: Ripser (Bauer 2021, near-optimal), Gudhi, Dionysus 2.

3. Persistence diagrams as metric spaces

Diagrams are compared via the bottleneck distance $d_B$ (worst-case matching to the diagonal) or the Wasserstein-$p$ distance $W_p$ (optimal transport cost). This equips the space of diagrams with well-defined geometry, though it is not a Hilbert space, complicating statistical inference.

4. Vectorization for ML pipelines

Raw diagrams don't live in $\mathbb{R}^n$, so integration with standard ML requires vectorization: persistence images (Adams et al.), persistence landscapes (Bubenik, provably stable in $L^p$), or kernel methods (sliced Wasserstein, persistence Fisher kernel).

5. Relative and extended persistence

Sublevel-set persistence on a real-valued function $f: \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{R}$ is a special case where barcodes read directly as Morse-theoretic information: bars correspond to pairs of critical points, connecting TDA to Morse Theory and Reeb Graphs.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated

Active research threads (as of early 2026):

  • Multiparameter persistence — extending from a single filtration parameter to two or more (e.g., scale + density). The structure theorem fails; the algebraic landscape is richer and harder. Approximation via fibered barcodes and signed barcodes is ongoing.
  • Statistical foundations — bootstrap confidence sets for barcodes, hypothesis testing via permutation on Wasserstein distances.
  • Deep learning integration — differentiable topology layers (e.g., Topological Loss via cubical persistence) enabling end-to-end gradient flow through topological features.
  • Sheaf-theoretic generalizations — replacing homology with sheaf cohomology to encode richer local data on the filtration, connecting to Cellular Sheaves and network science.

Discussion Prompts

  1. The structure theorem for persistence modules relies on working over a field $\mathbb{F}$. What breaks — algebraically and computationally — when you use integer coefficients, and does it ever matter in practice?
  1. Persistence diagrams are stable under $\|\cdot\|_\infty$ perturbations of the filter function, but Wasserstein distances are sensitive to the number of points in the diagram. How should this inform your choice of distance when comparing datasets of different sizes or densities?
  1. Multiparameter persistence lacks a complete discrete invariant analogous to the barcode. Is this fundamentally a computational obstacle, an algebraic one, or both — and what would a "good enough" approximation theory look like?
