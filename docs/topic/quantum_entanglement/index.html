Quantum Entanglement✓ reviewed

Science · Physics  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

INFO: Definition
Quantum entanglement is a property of a composite quantum system where the joint state cannot be written as a tensor product of individual subsystem states. Formally, a bipartite state $|\psi\rangle \in \mathcal{H}_A \otimes \mathcal{H}_B$ is entangled if and only if there exist no $|\phi\rangle_A \in \mathcal{H}_A$, $|\chi\rangle_B \in \mathcal{H}_B$ such that $|\psi\rangle = |\phi\rangle_A \otimes |\chi\rangle_B$.

The canonical example is the singlet Bell state:

$$|\Psi^-\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(|\uparrow\rangle_A|\downarrow\rangle_B - |\downarrow\rangle_A|\uparrow\rangle_B\right)$$

Here the total spin is fixed at zero, yet neither particle has a definite spin prior to measurement. Measuring particle $A$ along any axis instantly determines the outcome for particle $B$ along that same axis — not because a signal propagated, but because the global wavefunction collapses as a whole.

The reduced density matrix of subsystem $A$ is obtained by tracing out $B$: $\rho_A = \text{Tr}_B(|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)$. For the singlet, $\rho_A = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{I}$ — a maximally mixed state — which quantifies maximal entanglement via the von Neumann entropy $S(\rho_A) = -\text{Tr}(\rho_A \ln \rho_A) = \ln 2$.

Why It Matters

Entanglement is not merely a curiosity — it is a resource that enables strictly super-classical information processing:

  • Quantum cryptography (QKD): Entanglement-based protocols (e.g., E91) guarantee eavesdropping detection via Bell inequality violations.
  • Quantum teleportation: Transmits an unknown qubit state using only an entangled pair and two classical bits.
  • Quantum computing: Entanglement underlies the exponential Hilbert-space structure that makes algorithms like Shor's and Grover's tractable.
  • Condensed matter: Entanglement entropy diagnoses topological order, criticality, and area-law vs. volume-law phases — deeply connected to tensor network methods (MPS, PEPS).
  • Holography / AdS-CFT: Spatial geometry in the bulk may emerge from entanglement structure in the boundary CFT (Ryu–Takayanagi formula: $S = \text{Area}/4G_N$).
TIP: The Real Weirdness
Entanglement doesn't allow FTL communication — correlations only become visible when results are compared via a classical channel. The genuine strangeness is ontological: quantum systems lack definite local properties before joint measurement. Bell's theorem rules out any local hidden-variable completion, making this irreducibly non-classical.

Key Details

  1. Bell's Theorem and CHSH Inequality. Local hidden-variable theories satisfy $|S_{\text{CHSH}}| \leq 2$, where $S = \langle AB\rangle - \langle AB'\rangle + \langle A'B\rangle + \langle A'B'\rangle$. Quantum mechanics achieves the Tsirelson bound $2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.83$, experimentally confirmed with loophole-free Bell tests (e.g., Delft 2015, NIST 2015).
  1. Entanglement is basis-independent but measurement-basis-sensitive. The degree of correlations observed depends on the measurement axes chosen; only specific axis combinations violate Bell inequalities maximally.
  1. Mixed-state entanglement is harder. For mixed states, the separability criterion is non-trivial. The PPT criterion (Peres–Horodecki) provides a necessary condition: if $\rho^{T_B} \not\geq 0$, the state is entangled. In higher dimensions, PPT-entangled (bound-entangled) states exist — entangled but not distillable.
  1. Monogamy of entanglement. If $A$ is maximally entangled with $B$, it cannot be entangled with $C$ at all: $E(A:B) + E(A:C) \leq 1$ (in appropriate units). This underpins quantum cryptographic security.
  1. Entanglement entropy and area laws. Ground states of gapped local Hamiltonians obey an area law: $S \propto |\partial V|$ rather than volume. This is what makes tensor networks (MPS, MERA) efficient representations — and connects to why topological phases can be characterized by short-range entanglement.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated
Experimental platforms (photons, ions, superconducting qubits, neutral atoms) are advancing rapidly; specific fidelity benchmarks and record entanglement distances shift frequently.

Recent research directions of note:

  • Topological phases and entanglement structure: A March 2026 arXiv paper on Critical Topological Flat Bands (Li et al.) explicitly connects filled band states to short-range entangled topological states with power-law correlations, admitting exact tensor-network representations. This is a frontier where entanglement theory directly informs condensed matter.
  • Majorana zero modes: Probing non-Abelian anyons (relevant to topological quantum computing) relies on fermion-parity switches — themselves a form of protected entanglement between topological qubits.
  • Neutrino oscillations as quantum coherence: The factorization vs. non-factorization debate in neutrino S-matrix treatments touches on how quantum correlations between production and detection events (a cousin of entanglement) generate measurable non-factorizable corrections at the ~1% level.
  • Entanglement-based networks: Satellite QKD (Micius) and metropolitan fiber networks are moving toward entanglement distribution at scale, targeting quantum repeaters to overcome photon loss.

Discussion Prompts

  1. Entanglement entropy obeys an area law in gapped phases but a logarithmic correction appears at criticality ($S \sim \frac{c}{3}\ln L$ for 1+1D CFTs). What does this tell us about the structure of information near a quantum phase transition, and how does it constrain which states tensor networks can efficiently represent?

If entanglement cannot transmit information faster than light, yet quantum gravity proposals like ER=EPR suggest entangled black holes are geometrically connected by wormholes, what is the precise sense in which "non-locality" is or isn't present — and does the holographic picture dissolve the tension or merely relocate it?
