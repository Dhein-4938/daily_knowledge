Quantum Mechanics✓ reviewed

Science · Physics  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

INFO: Definition
Quantum mechanics is the fundamental physical theory governing matter and radiation at atomic and subatomic scales. Its mathematical backbone is the wave function $\psi(\mathbf{r}, t)$, a complex-valued field whose squared modulus gives a probability density: $|\psi|^2 = \rho$.

The time evolution of a closed system is governed by the Schrödinger equation:

$$i\hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial t}|\psi\rangle = \hat{H}|\psi\rangle$$

where $\hat{H}$ is the Hamiltonian operator. Observables correspond to Hermitian operators; measurement collapses the state onto an eigenstate with probability given by the Born rule:

$$P(a_n) = |\langle a_n | \psi \rangle|^2$$

Crucially, quantum systems occupy superpositions of eigenstates until measured — the machinery behind both quantum computing and deep interpretive controversy.

Why It Matters

Quantum mechanics isn't merely a correction to classical physics — it's the substrate. Every semiconductor, laser, MRI machine, and chemical bond depends on it. Its reach spans:

  • Quantum chemistry: molecular orbital theory, DFT, reaction dynamics
  • Quantum information: qubits, entanglement as a resource, error correction
  • Quantum field theory: the Standard Model, Feynman path integrals, renormalization
  • Emergent phenomena: superconductivity, topological phases, fractional quantum Hall effect
TIP: Classical mechanics as a limit
Classical mechanics is not simply "wrong" — it emerges from QM via the correspondence principle and decoherence. When $\hbar / S \to 0$ (action $S \gg \hbar$), quantum interference washes out and Newton's laws resurface. This is why macroscopic objects behave classically without contradiction.

Key Details

  1. Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle — Not a measurement disturbance artifact but a fundamental property of non-commuting observables. For position and momentum:

$$\sigma_x \sigma_p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}$$

Formally, for any two observables $\hat{A}, \hat{B}$: $\sigma_A \sigma_B \geq \frac{1}{2}|\langle[\hat{A},\hat{B}]\rangle|$.

  1. Wave–Particle Duality — Single particles (electrons, photons, even $C_{60}$ buckyballs) produce interference patterns in double-slit experiments, yet register as discrete impacts. The which-path information destroys the fringes — a manifestation of complementarity.
  1. Entanglement — A multi-particle state $|\Psi\rangle \neq |\psi_A\rangle \otimes |\psi_B\rangle$ is entangled. Bell's theorem (1964) proved no local hidden-variable theory can reproduce QM's predictions — experimentally confirmed to high precision by Aspect (1982) and loophole-free tests (2015).
  1. Quantization of Energy — Bound systems (particle in a box, hydrogen atom) have discrete spectra. For the hydrogen atom: $E_n = -\frac{13.6\,\text{eV}}{n^2}$. This discreteness was the historical trigger for the entire theory.
  1. Path Integral Formulation — Feynman's equivalent reformulation sums over all classical paths weighted by $e^{iS/\hbar}$. Interference between paths recovers quantum amplitudes; stationary phase recovers classical trajectories. Deeply connects QM to statistical mechanics via Wick rotation $t \to -i\tau$.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated

Active frontiers as of mid-2026:

  • Topological quantum matter: The bosonic Pfaffian quantum Hall state (Moore-Read state) has been realized in ultracold $^{87}$Rb atoms with Floquet-engineered synthetic magnetic fields. Non-Abelian anyons — whose braiding operations are topologically protected — are a major target for fault-tolerant quantum computing. Direct experimental access to pairing structure via multi-point density correlations is a new capability.
  • Quantum simulation of many-body physics: Collective neutrino oscillations in dense astrophysical environments (supernovae, neutron star mergers) are being mapped onto quantum circuits. Resource estimates show mid-range gate costs; efficient fermion-to-qubit encodings (Jordan-Wigner vs. Bravyi-Kitaev) are a live optimization problem.
  • Quantum light sources and nanoemitters: Colloidal quantum shells show near-Gaussian distributions of biexciton emission efficiencies ($\bar{\eta} \approx 0.55$, $\sigma \approx 0.12$), with Auger quenching scaling with particle volume. High-throughput SPAD-array photon correlation enables statistical characterization of nanoparticle ensembles — relevant to single-photon source engineering.

Discussion Prompts

  1. Measurement problem: The Schrödinger equation is unitary and deterministic, yet measurement produces definite outcomes probabilistically. Does decoherence solve the measurement problem, or merely relocate it — and does the choice of interpretation (Copenhagen, Many-Worlds, relational QM) have any empirically testable consequence?
  1. Non-Abelian anyons: The Pfaffian state supports excitations with non-Abelian exchange statistics — braiding particle $A$ around $B$ enacts a unitary matrix, not just a phase. What are the concrete gate operations implementable via braiding, and why does topological protection not eliminate all sources of error?
  1. Computational hardness: Simulating a generic $n$-qubit quantum system requires $2^n$ classical amplitudes. Yet quantum chemistry (a subset of QM) is already well-approximated by CCSD(T) for small molecules. Where exactly does the exponential hardness kick in, and is it always present or only in highly entangled regimes?

CRISPR-Cas9✓ reviewed

Science · Genomics  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

INFO: Definition
CRISPR-Cas9 is a two-component genome editing system: a guide RNA (gRNA) that provides sequence specificity, and Cas9, an endonuclease that executes a double-strand break (DSB) at the targeted locus.

The gRNA is a synthetic fusion of two originally distinct RNAs — the crRNA (CRISPR RNA, ~20 nt targeting sequence) and the tracrRNA (trans-activating crRNA, which scaffolds Cas9 binding). Cas9 scans DNA for a protospacer adjacent motif (PAM) — for S. pyogenes Cas9, the canonical sequence is 5′-NGG-3′ — then unwinds the duplex and interrogates the adjacent strand for Watson-Crick complementarity with the guide.

$$ \text{Target specificity} \approx \underbrace{20\text{ nt gRNA}}_{\sim 4\times10^{12}\text{ possible sequences}} \cap \text{PAM context} $$

Upon R-loop formation and full complementarity, Cas9's HNH domain cleaves the matched strand and the RuvC domain cleaves the non-matched strand, producing a blunt-ended DSB ~3 bp upstream of the PAM.

Why It Matters

The cell's DSB repair machinery drives the edit outcome:

  • NHEJ (non-homologous end joining) — error-prone, introduces indels → gene knockouts
  • HDR (homology-directed repair) — precise, requires a donor template → exact substitutions or insertions

This means CRISPR-Cas9 is simultaneously a disruption tool and a precision rewriting tool, depending on context. Applications span:

  • Functional genomics: genome-scale knockout screens (Cas9 + sgRNA libraries)
  • Therapeutics: ex vivo editing of HSCs for sickle-cell disease; in vivo delivery for transthyretin amyloidosis
  • Agriculture: disease-resistant crops without transgenesis
  • Diagnostics: SHERLOCK/DETECTR leverage Cas13/Cas12 collateral cleavage activity
TIP: Unexpected depth
The natural CRISPR system in bacteria is an adaptive immune memory — phage-derived spacers are heritable, acquired, and read out in real-time. Doudna and Charpentier's insight was that the crRNA:tracrRNA:Cas9 machinery could be reprogrammed with an entirely synthetic target, decoupling the recognition logic from the cutting machinery in a modular way that restriction enzymes never offered.

Key Details

  1. PAM constraint is the practical bottleneck. SpCas9 requires NGG every ~8 bp in the human genome on average, but targeting precision (especially for base editing) demands PAMs near the exact locus of interest. Engineered variants (SpRY, xCas9) relax PAM requirements at the cost of some specificity.
  1. Off-target cleavage is real but quantifiable. Mismatches in the seed region (PAM-proximal ~12 nt) are highly intolerant; distal mismatches are tolerated, especially with bulges. Assays like GUIDE-seq, CIRCLE-seq, and DISCOVER-Seq provide genome-wide off-target maps.
  1. Delivery is often the rate-limiting step. Cas9 protein (~160 kDa) + gRNA must reach target tissue. RNP electroporation works ex vivo; AAV imposes a ~4.7 kb packaging limit, requiring split-intein Cas9 strategies for in vivo; LNPs are emerging as non-viral alternatives.
  1. Base editors and prime editors extend the vocabulary. CBEs (cytosine base editors) and ABEs (adenine base editors) fuse a deaminase to nCas9 (nickase), enabling C→T or A→G transitions without a DSB. Prime editing uses a reverse transcriptase + pegRNA for all 12 transition/transversion types plus small indels.
WARNING
Note: this information may be dated — the base/prime editor landscape and clinical trial results evolve rapidly; verify current FDA/EMA status of specific therapies before citing.
  1. Epigenome editing is an emerging frontier. Catalytically dead Cas9 (dCas9) fused to KRAB, p300, DNMT3A, or TET enzymes allows locus-specific methylation, acetylation, or demethylation without altering the underlying sequence.

Current State

WARNING
Note: this information may be dated — active clinical and regulatory developments move quickly in this field.
  • Vertex/CRISPR Therapeutics' Casgevy (exa-cel) received FDA approval in late 2023 for sickle-cell disease — the first approved CRISPR therapy.
  • In vivo liver-targeting via LNPs has reached Phase I/II trials.
  • Large-scale saturation genome editing screens (e.g., at the Wellcome Sanger Institute) are systematically mapping gene essentiality, increasingly paired with automated cell counting pipelines to handle throughput.
  • Multiplex editing (simultaneous multi-locus cuts) and base editing in vivo remain active research frontiers.

Black Holes✓ reviewed

Science · Astrophysics  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravitational curvature becomes so extreme that the escape velocity exceeds $c$. This is not merely a density threshold — it is a topological feature of the spacetime manifold itself.

INFO: Event Horizon
The event horizon is a null hypersurface: a one-way causal boundary from which no future-directed timelike or null geodesic can escape to infinity. Crossing it is locally undetectable (no "wall"), but globally irreversible.

The Schwarzschild radius for a non-rotating black hole encodes this threshold:

$$r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2}$$

For a solar-mass object, $r_s \approx 3\,\text{km}$. The full solution space is richer: the Kerr metric adds angular momentum $J$, the Reissner–Nordström adds charge $Q$. The no-hair theorem asserts that these three parameters $(M, J, Q)$ uniquely characterise any stationary black hole exterior — a profound compression of astrophysical complexity.

At the centre lies a singularity where spacetime curvature diverges ($R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}R^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} \to \infty$), signalling the breakdown of classical GR rather than a physical infinity.

Why It Matters

Black holes sit at the intersection of GR, quantum field theory, thermodynamics, and information theory — making them the canonical stress-test for any theory of quantum gravity.

  • Astrophysically: they are the engines of AGN, quasars, and relativistic jets; supermassive black holes ($10^6$–$10^{10}\,M_\odot$) are apparently co-evolutionary with their host galaxies (the $M$–$\sigma$ relation).
  • As observational anchors: Sagittarius A ($4.3 \times 10^6\,M_\odot$) and M87 provided the first direct imaging via the Event Horizon Telescope, confirming shadow morphology predicted by GR.
  • Gravitational wave astronomy: merging compact objects are the primary LIGO/Virgo source class, enabling mass-function and spin measurements at cosmological distances.
TIP: Thermodynamic Analogy
The four laws of black hole mechanics map exactly onto thermodynamic laws, with surface gravity $\kappa$ playing the role of temperature and horizon area $A$ as entropy. This is not mere analogy — Hawking's derivation grounds it in QFT on curved spacetime.

Key Details

  1. Hawking Radiation — A black hole emits thermal radiation at temperature $T_H = \hbar c^3 / (8\pi G M k_B)$. Evaporation timescale scales as $t_{\text{evap}} \propto M^3$, making stellar-mass black holes effectively immortal on cosmological timescales. Primordial black holes of mass $\lesssim 10^{12}\,\text{kg}$ would have evaporated by now.
  1. Information Paradox — Hawking radiation is thermal, apparently destroying quantum information. Whether unitarity is preserved via subtle correlations in the radiation, firewalls at the horizon, or non-perturbative effects (Page curve via island formula) remains unresolved and is among the most active problems in theoretical physics.
  1. Penrose Process & Ergosphere — For Kerr black holes, the ergosphere (outside the event horizon) permits energy extraction from the rotation itself. The Blandford–Znajek mechanism is thought to power astrophysical jets via magnetic coupling to the ergosphere.
  1. Formation Channels — Stellar-mass: core collapse of $M \gtrsim 20\,M_\odot$ stars. Supermassive: pathways include direct collapse of massive gas clouds at high redshift, hierarchical mergers, and accretion — though the seeding mechanism remains observationally unconstrained.
  1. Tidal Disruption Events (TDEs) — Stars passing within the tidal radius of a supermassive black hole are disrupted; roughly half the mass accretes on a characteristic $t^{-5/3}$ fallback curve, providing a clean probe of quiescent black hole demographics.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated

Active research fronts include:

  • Black hole imaging: EHT has resolved Sgr A and M87; next-generation arrays (ngEHT) aim for time-resolved movies of the photon ring, testing GR in the strong-field regime.
  • Gravitational wave population statistics: LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA O4 and beyond are mapping the black hole mass function, probing the pair-instability gap (~50–130 $M_\odot$) and intermediate-mass black holes.
  • Page curve and island formula: recent work using replica wormholes and the Ryu-Takayanagi formula in JT gravity reproduces the unitary Page curve, suggesting information is ultimately preserved — but the mechanism in 4D gravity remains unclear.
  • Primordial black holes as dark matter: tightly constrained by microlensing and CMB, but mass windows near $10^{-12}\,M_\odot$ remain viable.

Protein Folding✓ reviewed

Science · Biochemistry  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

Protein folding is the physical process by which a linear polypeptide chain — synthesized ribosome-side as a sequence of amino acids — spontaneously collapses into a precise, thermodynamically stable three-dimensional structure called the native state. This transition from disordered random coil to ordered globule is driven by a complex interplay of hydrophobic collapse, backbone hydrogen bonding, electrostatic interactions, and entropic forces.

INFO: Anfinsen's Dogma
The native state is fully determined by the primary structure (amino acid sequence) alone, under physiological conditions. Formally: sequence → structure → function. This was demonstrated by Anfinsen's ribonuclease refolding experiments (Nobel Prize, 1972) and remains the foundational axiom of the field.

The thermodynamic driving force is minimization of free energy. The native state sits at the global minimum of the folding energy landscape:

$$\Delta G_\text{fold} = \Delta H_\text{fold} - T\Delta S_\text{fold} < 0$$

where the enthalpic gain (hydrophobic burial, H-bonds) must overcome the conformational entropy cost of ordering the chain. Typical stability margins are surprisingly small — only ~5–60 kJ/mol, meaning proteins are marginally stable by design.

Why It Matters

Protein function is inseparable from three-dimensional structure. Enzymes, receptors, ion channels, antibodies — all derive their specificity from precise geometric complementarity. Misfolding is therefore not merely a structural failure; it is a functional catastrophe.

Disease relevance is substantial: Alzheimer's (Aβ amyloid), Parkinson's (α-synuclein), Huntington's (polyglutamine aggregates), and prion diseases all involve the accumulation of misfolded β-sheet-rich amyloid fibrils. Prions are a uniquely alarming case — a misfolded protein that catalyzes misfolding in native copies, propagating without nucleic acid involvement.

Beyond pathology, protein folding underpins rational drug design, synthetic biology, and enzyme engineering. Predicting fold from sequence was called "the most important unsolved problem in biology" for decades — its resolution via AlphaFold2 (2021) represents one of the most significant advances in modern science.

Key Details

  1. Levinthal's Paradox: A 100-residue protein has ~$3^{100}$ backbone conformations. Random search would take longer than the age of the universe. Yet folding occurs in milliseconds. Resolution: folding follows a funnel-shaped energy landscape, not random search — local contacts nucleate structure progressively.
  1. Folding timescales span ~10 orders of magnitude: ultrafast single-domain proteins fold in microseconds; proline isomerization-limited proteins take minutes. The Folding@home project mapped this kinetically using distributed computing.
  1. Cotranslational folding: Folding often begins during ribosomal translation, before the chain is complete. This means the N-terminal domain folds in a different context (and sometimes to a different intermediate) than it would in isolation.
  1. Chaperones don't encode structure: Molecular chaperones (e.g., HSP70, GroEL/GroES) prevent aggregation and provide a sequestered environment for folding but do not specify the native structure. They rescue kinetically trapped intermediates.
  1. Intrinsically disordered proteins (IDPs): A significant fraction of the eukaryotic proteome lacks a well-defined native fold. IDPs are functional because of their disorder — enabling promiscuous binding, post-translational modification hubs, and entropic bristles. This partially invalidates Anfinsen's dogma as a universal rule.
TIP: Contact Order Predicts Folding Rate
Proteins with predominantly local contacts (low contact order) fold faster than those with many long-range contacts. This is because short-range interactions form more rapidly during the stochastic search, creating nucleation seeds.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated

AlphaFold2 (DeepMind, 2021) essentially solved single-chain structure prediction from sequence, achieving experimental accuracy on the CASP14 benchmark. AlphaFold3 (2024) extended this to protein–nucleic acid and protein–small molecule complexes. The PDB now has hundreds of millions of predicted structures via the AlphaFold Protein Structure Database.

Open challenges include: de novo design of novel folds (ProteinMPNN, RFdiffusion), predicting conformational ensembles rather than single structures, understanding folding kinetics computationally, and modeling the folding of intrinsically disordered regions under cellular crowding conditions.
