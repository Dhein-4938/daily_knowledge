General Relativity✓ reviewed

Science · Physics  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

General Relativity (GR) is Einstein's geometric theory of gravitation (1916), which reframes gravity not as a force but as the curvature of four-dimensional spacetime caused by mass-energy distributions.

INFO: Einstein Field Equations (EFE)
The core relation is encoded in the EFE: $$G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu}$$ where $G_{\mu\nu} = R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}Rg_{\mu\nu}$ is the Einstein tensor (encoding curvature), $T_{\mu\nu}$ is the stress-energy tensor (encoding matter/radiation content), $\Lambda$ is the cosmological constant, and $g_{\mu\nu}$ is the metric tensor. These are 10 coupled, nonlinear, second-order PDEs.

Wheeler's aphorism distills the mutual dependence elegantly: "Space-time tells matter how to move; matter tells space-time how to curve." Free-falling objects follow geodesics — the straightest possible paths through curved spacetime — which appear as gravitational trajectories to an outside observer.

Why It Matters

GR is the foundational framework for virtually all of modern astrophysics and cosmology:

  • Cosmology: The Friedmann equations (derived from EFE) describe an expanding universe, underpinning the Big Bang model and the existence of the cosmic microwave background.
  • Black holes: Singularities emerge as mathematical predictions; stellar-mass and supermassive black holes are now observationally confirmed via LIGO, Event Horizon Telescope, etc.
  • GPS & navigation: Gravitational time dilation at satellite altitudes (~45 μs/day gain) must be corrected — without GR, GPS would drift ~10 km/day.
  • Gravitational waves: Ripples in spacetime, first directly detected by LIGO in 2015 (GW150914), opened an entirely new observational channel.
TIP: GR as a limit of something deeper
GR reduces to Newtonian gravity in the weak-field, slow-motion limit — yet it remains irreducible to quantum mechanics. This is not a flaw but a clue: GR is almost certainly an effective field theory valid below the Planck scale ($\ell_P \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-35}$ m), not a final theory.

Key Details

  1. Equivalence Principle: The local indistinguishability of inertial and gravitational mass is GR's foundational postulate. It implies that locally, any curved spacetime looks flat (Minkowski), and gravitational effects vanish in free fall.
  1. Geodesic deviation: The relative acceleration of nearby geodesics is governed by the Riemann curvature tensor $R^\rho{}_{\sigma\mu\nu}$, making it the invariant measure of tidal gravity. Flat spacetime $\Leftrightarrow$ $R^\rho{}_{\sigma\mu\nu} = 0$ everywhere.
  1. Schwarzschild metric: The unique vacuum, spherically symmetric solution:

$$ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{r_s}{r}\right)c^2 dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{r_s}{r}\right)^{-1}dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega^2$$

where $r_s = 2GM/c^2$ is the Schwarzschild radius. This predicts light bending, gravitational redshift, and the existence of an event horizon.

  1. Gravitational waves: Linearized GR admits wave solutions propagating at $c$. The quadrupole formula gives radiated power $P \propto \dddot{Q}_{ij}\dddot{Q}^{ij}$, where $Q_{ij}$ is the mass quadrupole moment — monopole and dipole radiation are forbidden by conservation laws.
  1. Causality and singularities: The Penrose–Hawking singularity theorems prove that singularities are generic under reasonable energy conditions, not artifacts of symmetry. This is where GR predicts its own breakdown.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated

The quantum gravity problem remains entirely open. Leading candidate frameworks include:

  • Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG): Quantizes spacetime geometry itself; predicts a discrete area/volume spectrum at the Planck scale.
  • String Theory: Embeds gravity in a higher-dimensional framework; contains a spin-2 graviton naturally but requires supersymmetry and extra dimensions.
  • Causal Dynamical Triangulations and asymptotic safety: More conservative approaches seeking a UV-complete quantum field theory of gravity.

On the observational frontier, multi-messenger astronomy (gravitational waves + EM + neutrinos) is actively stress-testing GR in strong-field regimes. Deviations from GR in the merger ringdown phase of binary black holes — parameterized via quasi-normal modes — are an active search target with next-generation detectors (Einstein Telescope, LISA).

The cosmological constant problem — why $\Lambda$ is ~120 orders of magnitude smaller than naive quantum field theory estimates — remains one of the deepest unsolved problems in physics.

Quantum Entanglement✓ reviewed

Science · Physics  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

INFO: Definition
Quantum entanglement is a correlation between composite quantum systems such that the joint state cannot be written as a tensor product of individual states. For a bipartite system, a pure state $|\psi\rangle \in \mathcal{H}_A \otimes \mathcal{H}_B$ is entangled if and only if: $$|\psi\rangle \neq |\phi\rangle_A \otimes |\chi\rangle_B$$ for any $|\phi\rangle_A \in \mathcal{H}_A$, $|\chi\rangle_B \in \mathcal{H}_B$.

The canonical example is the Bell state (one of four maximally entangled two-qubit states):

$$|\Phi^+\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(|00\rangle + |11\rangle\right)$$

Measuring qubit $A$ in the computational basis instantly fixes the outcome distribution for qubit $B$, regardless of spatial separation. Entanglement is quantified via entanglement entropy: for a bipartite pure state with reduced density matrix $\rho_A = \text{Tr}_B(|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)$,

$$S(\rho_A) = -\text{Tr}(\rho_A \log_2 \rho_A)$$

$S = 0$ for product states; $S = 1$ ebit for maximally entangled qubit pairs.

Why It Matters

Entanglement is the resource that makes quantum information genuinely distinct from classical information theory. It underpins:

  • Quantum key distribution (e.g., Ekert E91 protocol) — eavesdropping disturbs correlations detectably
  • Quantum teleportation — transmitting an unknown qubit state using 2 classical bits + 1 shared ebit
  • Quantum computing — entanglement between qubits enables exponential parallelism in algorithms like Shor's
  • Condensed matter — area laws for entanglement entropy characterize topological phases and guide tensor-network simulations
  • Holography / AdS-CFT — Ryu–Takayanagi formula relates bulk geometry to boundary entanglement entropy, hinting that spacetime itself may be entanglement-structured
TIP: Insight
Entanglement is not transmitted between particles post-creation — it is a property of the joint Hilbert space. The non-local correlations carry no causal signal, which is why faster-than-light communication remains impossible despite the spooky correlations.

Key Details

1. Bell's Theorem and the end of local hidden variables

Bell (1964) derived inequalities that any local realistic theory must satisfy. Experiments (Aspect 1982, loophole-free tests 2015) show Nature violates them. The CHSH inequality is:

$$\left|\langle AB\rangle + \langle AB'\rangle + \langle A'B\rangle - \langle A'B'\rangle\right| \leq 2 \quad \text{(classical bound)}$$

Quantum mechanics achieves the Tsirelson bound of $2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.83$.

2. Nonlocality without entanglement (NLWE)

Separable (unentangled) states can still exhibit nonlocality in the operational sense: Bennett et al. (1999) showed that certain orthogonal product-state bases cannot be perfectly distinguished by local operations and classical communication (LOCC). Recent work (Lee & Bae, 2026) extends this via maximum-confidence discrimination, showing global measurements outperform separable ones even without entanglement — and enabling semi-device-independent certification.

3. Mixed-state entanglement and PPT criterion

For mixed states $\rho_{AB}$, separability means $\rho = \sum_i p_i \rho_A^i \otimes \rho_B^i$. The Peres–Horodecki (PPT) criterion: if $\rho_{AB}$ is separable, its partial transpose has non-negative eigenvalues. PPT is necessary and sufficient in $2\times 2$ and $2\times 3$ dimensions, but not beyond — bound entangled states exist that are PPT yet inseparable.

4. Monogamy of entanglement

Entanglement cannot be freely shared. For three qubits $A$, $B$, $C$, the Coffman–Kundu–Wootters (CKW) inequality constrains:

$$C^2_{A|BC} \geq C^2_{AB} + C^2_{AC}$$

where $C$ is the concurrence. This monogamy is crucial for quantum cryptography security proofs.

5. Decoherence as the practical barrier

Entanglement is fragile: interaction with the environment causes decoherence, rapidly collapsing superpositions. Preserving entanglement across macroscopic distances requires extreme isolation (optical fibers, vacuum links) or error-correcting codes.

  • NLWE certification — semi-device-independent protocols (Lee & Bae 2026) enable experimental NLWE demonstration with realistic detection efficiencies, bypassing the need for perfect detectors
  • Entanglement in topological systems — proximity-induced spin-orbit coupling in moiré graphene (QAH systems) is revealing how topological edge states relate to magnetic entanglement structure
  • Satellite-based entanglement distribution — China's Micius satellite demonstrated entangled photon pairs over ~1,200 km; global quantum networks are a near-term target
  • Tensor networks and holography — entanglement entropy as a probe of quantum gravity remains an intense theoretical frontier (ER = EPR conjecture by Maldacena & Susskind)

Transformer Architecture✓ reviewed

Tech · Machine Learning  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

INFO
A Transformer is a sequence-to-sequence neural architecture that dispenses entirely with recurrence and convolution, instead relying on scaled dot-product attention to model dependencies between all positions simultaneously.

The fundamental operation is Multi-Head Self-Attention. Given input matrix $X \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d}$, each head computes:

$$\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V$$

where $Q = XW^Q$, $K = XW^K$, $V = XW^V$ are learned projections. Multiple heads run in parallel, their outputs concatenated and re-projected:

$$\text{MHA}(X) = \text{Concat}(\text{head}_1, \ldots, \text{head}_h)W^O$$

A Transformer block wraps MHA with residual connections, layer normalization, and a position-wise FFN (typically two linear layers with a GELU/ReLU nonlinearity). Since attention is permutation-invariant, positional encodings — sinusoidal, learned, or rotary (RoPE) — are injected to convey sequence order.

Why It Matters

Transformers unified NLP, vision, audio, and multimodal modeling under a single inductive-bias-light framework. Their $O(n^2)$ attention cost is the central scalability bottleneck, but their parallelism across positions (unlike RNNs) makes them GPU/TPU-friendly at training time. The architecture underlies GPT, BERT, T5, ViT, Whisper, AlphaFold 2, and virtually every frontier model as of 2026.

TIP
The FFN sublayer (often 4× wider than the residual stream) acts as a key-value memory — individual neurons store factual associations. This explains why scaling parameters in FFN layers improves knowledge retrieval more than scaling attention heads alone.

Key Details

  1. Residual stream as communication bus. Each layer reads from and writes to the same $d_\text{model}$-dimensional residual stream. Mechanistic interpretability frames this as parallel "circuits" adding their contributions, making superposition the dominant compression strategy.
  1. Positional encoding trade-offs. Sinusoidal (absolute) encodings generalize poorly beyond training length. Relative encodings (ALiBi, RoPE) enable better length extrapolation; RoPE encodes position as a rotation in complex space, achieving $\text{Re}[q e^{im\theta} \cdot (k e^{in\theta})^*] = \text{Re}[qk^* e^{i(m-n)\theta}]$, making attention scores depend only on relative offset $m-n$.
  1. Attention sink phenomenon. Empirically, the first token (often <BOS>) accumulates disproportionate attention mass across many heads — a consequence of softmax needing to "dump" probability somewhere when all content is irrelevant. Streaming inference systems (e.g., StreamingLLM) exploit this by always retaining the sink token in the KV cache.
  1. KV cache and inference cost. Autoregressive decoding materializes a KV cache of size $O(n \cdot n_\text{layers} \cdot 2d)$ per sequence. This is frequently the binding memory constraint at inference, motivating grouped-query attention (GQA) and multi-query attention (MQA) which share key/value heads across query heads.
  1. Training instability at scale. Pre-LayerNorm (applying norm before the sublayer) is more stable than post-LN for large models; gradient norms in post-LN grow with depth and require careful warmup. Most modern LLMs use pre-LN or RMSNorm variants.

Current State

WARNING
Note: this information may be dated

Active research fronts as of mid-2026 include reasoning-aware retrieval augmentation: rather than grounding a Transformer in semantically similar documents, frameworks like RA-RFT train retrievers to surface analogous reasoning patterns, improving mathematical reasoning benchmarks significantly over standard RLHF/GRPO fine-tuning. This signals a shift from treating retrieval as a knowledge lookup to treating it as a cognitive scaffold.

Separately, knowledge graph integration (e.g., Agents-K1) aims to give Transformers structured access to entity-relation graphs extracted from millions of papers, enabling multi-hop scientific reasoning that flat token context windows handle poorly. Positional encoding research has also expanded to graph domains, where truncated spectral and walk-based PEs for GNNs are now understood to have fundamentally different expressive power under truncation — a finding that likely informs PE design for structured-input Transformers.

Diffusion Models✓ reviewed

Tech · Machine Learning  ·  ~3 min read

Core Concept

Diffusion models are a class of latent variable generative models that learn a data distribution by modeling a two-phase stochastic process: a fixed forward process that destroys structure, and a learned reverse process that reconstructs it.

INFO: Formal Definition
Let $\mathbf{x}_0 \sim q(\mathbf{x})$ be a data sample. The forward process adds Gaussian noise over $T$ timesteps: $$q(\mathbf{x}_t \mid \mathbf{x}_{t-1}) = \mathcal{N}\!\left(\mathbf{x}_t;\, \sqrt{1-\beta_t}\,\mathbf{x}_{t-1},\, \beta_t \mathbf{I}\right)$$ where $\{\beta_t\}$ is a variance schedule. As $t \to T$, $\mathbf{x}_T \approx \mathcal{N}(\mathbf{0}, \mathbf{I})$. The reverse process is learned: $$p_\theta(\mathbf{x}_{t-1} \mid \mathbf{x}_t) = \mathcal{N}\!\left(\mathbf{x}_{t-1};\, \boldsymbol{\mu}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, t),\, \boldsymbol{\Sigma}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, t)\right)$$ Training minimizes a variational lower bound (ELBO) on the negative log-likelihood, which reduces in practice to a denoising objective: predict the noise $\boldsymbol{\epsilon}$ added at each step.

Multiple equivalent formalisms unify under this umbrella: DDPMs (Ho et al. 2020), score-based models via Langevin dynamics (Song & Ermon), and continuous-time stochastic differential equations (Song et al. 2021), where the forward process becomes an Itô SDE and the reverse is governed by the score function $\nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \log p_t(\mathbf{x})$.

Why It Matters

Diffusion models resolved a long-standing quality/diversity trade-off in generative modeling. GANs produce sharp samples but suffer mode collapse and training instability; VAEs are stable but blurry. Diffusion models achieve state-of-the-art sample fidelity with tractable likelihoods and stable training.

Commercially, models like Stable Diffusion and DALL-E 3 combine a diffusion backbone with text encoders and cross-attention, enabling open-ended text-to-image synthesis. Beyond vision, the framework extends to audio (WaveGrad), molecular design (protein structure generation), and reinforcement learning (Diffuser for planning).

TIP: Unifying Insight
The score-matching perspective reveals that a denoising network $\boldsymbol{\epsilon}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, t)$ implicitly estimates the score $\nabla_{\mathbf{x}_t} \log p_t(\mathbf{x}_t)$. This connects diffusion models to classical energy-based models and Langevin MCMC — the generative process is gradient ascent on a learned log-density.

Key Details

  1. Noise Schedule: The choice of $\beta_t$ (linear, cosine, learned) dramatically affects sample quality and training dynamics. The cosine schedule (Nichol & Dhariwal 2021) improves low-frequency structure capture.
  1. Backbone Architecture: The denoising network is typically a U-Net with time-conditional normalization (GroupNorm + sinusoidal timestep embeddings) or a Diffusion Transformer (DiT), which replaces convolutional blocks with self-attention and scales more favorably.
  1. Sampling Speed: Naive DDPM requires $T \approx 1000$ NFEs (network function evaluations). DDIM (Song et al.) reformulates sampling deterministically, reducing to ~50 steps. Consistency models and flow matching push this further, enabling single-step generation.
  1. Latent Diffusion: Running diffusion in pixel space is expensive. Stable Diffusion operates in the latent space of a pretrained VAE, compressing $512{\times}512$ images to $64{\times}64{\times}4$ tensors — a ~48× compute reduction with minimal perceptual loss.
  1. Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG): Conditional generation without a separate classifier. The network is jointly trained with and without conditioning; at inference, outputs are extrapolated:

$$\tilde{\boldsymbol{\epsilon}}_\theta = \boldsymbol{\epsilon}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, \varnothing) + w\bigl(\boldsymbol{\epsilon}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, c) - \boldsymbol{\epsilon}_\theta(\mathbf{x}_t, \varnothing)\bigr)$$

where $w > 1$ trades diversity for adherence to condition $c$.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated
The field moves extremely fast; architectural frontiers and benchmark records shift monthly.

Active research lines as of mid-2026 include: flow matching (Lipman et al.) as a cleaner interpolation-based alternative to score matching; consistency distillation for near-real-time generation; video diffusion (Sora-class models) scaling temporal coherence; and integrating diffusion sampling into agentic pipelines (e.g., interleaved text-image generation, as in InterleaveThinker). Mechanistic interpretability of what timestep-specific representations encode is an open theoretical frontier.

Laws of Thermodynamics✓ reviewed

Science · Physics  ·  ~4 min read

Core Concept

The four laws of thermodynamics define the fundamental constraints on energy, entropy, and temperature in physical systems. They are empirical in origin — extracted from observation — yet carry the force of absolute physical prohibition.

INFO: The Four Laws
- Zeroth Law: Thermal equilibrium is transitive. If $A \sim C$ and $B \sim C$, then $A \sim B$. This bootstraps the concept of temperature as a well-defined scalar. - First Law: Energy is conserved. For a closed system: $\Delta U = Q - W$, where $U$ is internal energy, $Q$ is heat added, $W$ is work done by the system. - Second Law: Total entropy never decreases in an isolated system: $\Delta S_\text{universe} \geq 0$. Equality holds for reversible processes. - Third Law: As $T \to 0$, entropy approaches a constant (typically zero for perfect crystals): $\lim_{T \to 0} S = S_0$.

The second law is often stated via the Clausius inequality:

$$dS \geq \frac{\delta Q}{T}$$

with equality only on reversible paths. This inequality is doing enormous conceptual work — it encodes the arrow of time.

Why It Matters

These laws constrain every energy-converting process in the universe — from stellar fusion to biochemical metabolism to silicon logic gates. Their power is largely negative: they rule out entire classes of devices and processes without requiring detailed microscopic analysis.

TIP: Engineering as Applied Second Law
The Carnot efficiency $\eta_C = 1 - T_C/T_H$ is a direct consequence of the second law. No heat engine operating between two reservoirs can exceed it. Every real engine (Rankine, Brayton, Otto) is measured as a fraction of this ceiling — thermodynamics sets the target, engineering chases it.

The laws connect to Statistical Mechanics via Boltzmann's identification $S = k_B \ln \Omega$, grounding macroscopic entropy in the count of accessible microstates. This bridge explains why entropy increases: high-$\Omega$ states are overwhelmingly more probable.

Applications span:

  • Chemistry: Gibbs free energy $G = H - TS$ determines spontaneity and equilibrium
  • Cosmology: The thermodynamic arrow of time aligns with the cosmological arrow (low-entropy Big Bang)
  • Information theory: Shannon entropy is formally identical to Boltzmann entropy; Landauer's principle ties bit erasure to heat dissipation

Key Details

  1. The Zeroth Law was named retroactively. It was implicit in earlier work but only articulated after the first three laws were well-established — hence the awkward numbering.
  1. The Second Law is statistical, not absolute. Boltzmann's H-theorem derives entropy increase from kinetic theory, but Loschmidt's paradox (time-reversible microdynamics → irreversible macrodynamics) and Poincaré recurrence show the law holds only in the thermodynamic limit ($N \to \infty$). Fluctuations can locally violate it; the Fluctuation Theorem quantifies the probability of entropy-decreasing trajectories.
  1. The Third Law forbids reaching absolute zero. It takes infinite steps of adiabatic demagnetization (or any cooling cycle) to reach $T = 0$, because $\Delta S \to 0$ as $T \to 0$, making each step less effective. This is sometimes stated as a separate corollary: absolute zero is unattainable.
  1. Glasses violate the Third Law's entropy-zero corollary. Non-crystalline solids retain residual entropy at $T = 0$ due to frozen-in disorder — the system falls out of equilibrium before reaching the ground state. The law strictly applies to equilibrium systems.
  1. Work and heat are path-dependent. $\delta Q$ and $\delta W$ are inexact differentials (hence the $\delta$ notation), while $dU$ and $dS$ are exact. This distinction is frequently glossed over and causes persistent confusion in thermodynamic calculations.

Current State

WARNING: Note: this information may be dated

Active research frontiers include:

  • Quantum thermodynamics: Extending the laws to systems where quantum coherence and entanglement play a role. Questions around quantum work extractors, quantum heat engines (e.g., three-level masers), and whether coherence provides thermodynamic advantage remain open.
  • Non-equilibrium thermodynamics: Stochastic thermodynamics (Jarzynski equality, Crooks fluctuation theorem) extends the second law to small systems driven far from equilibrium — relevant for molecular motors and single-molecule experiments.
  • Black hole thermodynamics: The Bekenstein-Hawking entropy $S_{BH} = k_B A / 4\ell_P^2$ suggests spacetime itself obeys thermodynamic laws, hinting at a deep connection between gravity, Information Theory, and entropy.
  • Maxwell's Demon resolution: Landauer's erasure principle (erasing 1 bit dissipates $k_B T \ln 2$ of heat) closes the loophole — the demon's memory erasure always costs at least as much entropy as was apparently gained.
